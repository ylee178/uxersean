Unleashed Software

UX Designer • Product Design • 2016-2018
Background

Unleashed Software (UNL) is an inventory management SaaS product that I have been working on for 2 years. I have worked with 2 different teams in the company. Each team consists of a PO and 6-7 devs plus QA resource. Unleashed already had HTML prototypes for their brand guidelines, so my focus was on designing new features while conforming to the guidelines.

Design Challenge

Many UNL users use an exporting functionality and their files can massive (hundreds and thousands of product types and their related transactions, etc). Without a notification/management system, the users have to keep checking on the product if it is ready to download. The product team thought that an easy fix for this will be adding a small icon in the header and a number badge is displayed when there is an update for the users to look at regarding the exporting functionality (e.g. download ready, failed to load the request, processing, etc).

Design Iterations

The design options above have been tested internally (picked employees who are relatively less exposed to the product). One of the important points I wanted to test out was whether users will be able to understand the functionality without a text label. The result was interesting. Most people intuitively follow to click on the task icon as a number badge was formed as soon as they have requested exporting large data. Our team was convinced that the icon itself will be working for our real users without the label.
